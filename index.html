the problem

Hands-on travel planners have been inundated with overfilled and unfulfilled itineraries leading to less than ideal travel planning experiences.

the opportunity

Our app, tinit, plans to improve the travel planning experience by removing the clutter of over information, keep results relevant, and offer solutions for itinerary setting.

user research plan

Conduct a series of six user interviews to gain a qualitative understanding of the existing audience and their travel experiences. Then analyze the findings and break them down into affinity diagrams and empathy maps in order to come up with a user persona. At the same time, use these tools to pinpoint any pain points and areas of opportunities for development.

user insight

During the one-on-one interviews, we discovered that our users tend to cram too much into their itinerary with not enough time to do everything they had originally planned for. Therefore, we believe that we can help deliver a more satisfying trip experience if we gave users a platform with relevant activity details to more realistically organize their trips.

Feature prioritization matrix

Using the I Like, I Wish, What If method, certain features were determined to be of more importance based off the user insight and problem statement. These features were then put into a prioritization matrix to understand feasibility and ease of implementation.
